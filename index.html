    Dennis Berkholtz

    Commissioner,
    National Senior League (NSL)

    Dennis Berkholtz founded the National Senior League in 2009. As part of the business plan, he wanted to use the NSL and Wii bowling as a vehicle to raise money for Alzheimer's/dementia research in honor of his father, Lester Berkholtz, who died with dementia after suffering for over 5 years.

    Berkholtz has had an extensive background in marketing and implementation of high level funding programs. These range from working in the early stages of the building of the Georgia Dome to working with Olympic programs in Atlanta (1996) and Salt Lake City (2002). He was an Olympic athlete and coach in the sport of Team handball (1972 and 1976).

    About the NSL

    The National Senior League (NSL) brings independent senior living communitites, CCRC’s, assisted living communities, rehab centers, nursing homes, affordable housing communities and senior centers from across the country together for fun and competition.

    The NSL manages two championships each year. The Regional Championships begin in mid-February and end in mid-April. The National Championships begin in mid-August and end in mid-October. Championships are played in 7 divisions based on level of ability. For more information and registration visit the NSL website www.nslgames.com.

About the NSL-ALZ Foundation

The NSL-ALZ Foundation has 3 missions — (1) to raise money for Alzheimer's/dementia research; (2) to develop and support programs that educate seniors about the importance of Alzheimer's/dementia testing for early signs of the disease; and (3) to recognize and honor selected Alzheimer's/dementia caretakers with a vacation break from caretaking responsibilities.
